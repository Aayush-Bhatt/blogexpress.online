"Few go to school and few go to work, same age yet so much of difference"

But what about Indians, they do not have any insecurity from dark complexion still they are obsessed with ‘fair skin’, why? Well, in India it is more about how you look rather than what you are, these kinds of social norms led to the formation of uneasy principles in society and one of them is a higher demand of fair skin. Companies like fair and lovely have billion-dollar turnover every year by selling fake formulas of fairness and people are buying it because that’s what society demands. You can often see matrimonial ads with boldly stating “Gora Rang Hona Chahia” (we want a fair-skinned groom or bride). This inexplicable obsession for fairness led many people to put The general perception can be understood from the show business where you rarely find someone with the dark complexion to get big roles and is popular among the mainstream, why? Because people only want to watch a shiny and glaring skin artist appear on the screen. But this notion is slowly changing, the new generation now prefers acting skills over the skin skills. The fight for racism is unnecessary efforts on changing their skin colour so they can too fit well among the racist society. And all of this has become so common that people do not even think to oppose it, they have accepted this as the ultimate truth of life, an an unalterable fact which cannot be challenged by anyone. If we think that racism is because of a lack of knowledge and awareness than we are wrong! Educated people are more tilted towards racism even after being well aware of the the fact that skin colour is a result of cells called ‘melanin’ and this perception is quite terrifying. Discriminating and not liking someone because of their skin colour shows that 10 million years of human evolution has not brought sufficient sensibility in the humans to understand the notion that colour is nothing more then just ‘a colour’ and it does not define their nature, habits or attitude. going to be very long, it will take time to break the old traditions and customs, as a popular phrase in English says ‘Rome wasn't built in a day’, but with the consistent efforts of the new generation we can end this soon. The popular comedian Zakir khan(who by the is dark) said in one of his podcast, “Mai Jarra Hi Sahi Mera Wajood Toh Hai, Hua Kare Samandar Meri Talaash Mai” which means do not lose Four Minneapolis police officers on May 25th forgot to embrace the above quote when they choked down George Floyd, an American black for counterfeiting bills. This cruel event led people around the world to think about what Martin Luther King Jr. said about racism 60 years back. The fight for racism is not new, people from centuries had to suffer just because they have a darker complexion. If we go deep and think about it, racism is not just about skin colour discrimination rather it is a subtle way of saying that you are inferior to us in every manner, whether it is knowledge, class, appearance, and much more. Racism is a result of insecurity, jealousy, and opportunity crisis of so-called ‘white people’, who deep down is afraid of other races to take up their jobs and place in the society. You can find traces of racism around the globe but this is more prevalent among Americans, maybe because everyone is running towards America for a better future.? your identity because someone doesn’t like it, be the way you are and someone will cherish you.
